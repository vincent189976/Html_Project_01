A List of Vincent's Favourite Films

I have been an avid cinema lover for as long as I can remember. I watch films to understand the world better. Films bridge the gap between the absurdity of cinema & the mediocrity of reality. As Camus reflects, 'It is that divorce between the mind that desires & the world that disappoints, my nostalgia for unity, this fragmented universe & the contradiction that binds them together.' Below I have compiled a list of few of my favourite films. I would love to discuss them with you. You can contact me via Email. Thanks for checking out my webpage!

  1. Blade Runner (1982)

    This is my all time favourite film. Aside from its breathtaking neo-noir visuals & Scott's immaculate direction, it questions the veracity of humanity as well as the plausibility of a plastic identity.


    IMDB entry

  2. No Man's Land (1987)

    Set against the backdrop of neon-lit, smoky 80s Los Angeles, this film explores the moral paradox of an undercover cop who develops a close relationship with the leader of a car theft ring.


    IMDB entry

  3. Taxi Driver (1976)

    Scorsese explores the volatility of a crime-infested, drug-ridden 70s New York City through the eyes of a traumatized war veteran, Travis Bickle.
